Here follows a proposal to the Tactical Week:end in SESC Vila Mariana: an informal conversation that is able to allow and stimulate spontaneous interactions in a kind of real-virtual jam.

6 computers will be at the stage of the auditorium running linux, so that the public present, table participants and national and international guests can upload to a common environment images (.jpgs), animations (.swfs) and audios (.mp3). In fact, anyone connected to the web can upload material. This material will inhabitate the program Visitor's Studio from the group of British net.artists Furtherfield http://www.furtherfield.org. The idea is that the Brazilian tactical media crew apropiate this software to converse in images and sounds about relevant themes and that the *visit* can open the talks even further.

Each guest will develop with the other participants a dynamic for his/her table. This will be from inviting international guests to interact with them via Furtherfield's software to propose themes to be discussed.

To interact with the guests and the public, the address is> http://www.furtherstudio.org/live Please check the times of the tables! São Paulo, Brazil is (GMT -3).

How it works?

Furtherfield.org developed a live interactive software so that artists and colaborators can interact with each other. Register in 'Visitor's Studio' with a name, password and e-mail. This software will be used as a virtual environment to accomodate translocal and multimedia interaction between theparticipants and guests of the Tactical Week:end, present in são paulo or not.

Once in the site, you can interfere on all media material there. Click on "current mix" to modify archives online or in "upload a file" to send your material. There is also a chat. To publish a comment you have only to write and click "enter" and it will be visible to all guests and public.

There will also be an audio streaming of the presentations, on the Furtherfield site live and on this site.
